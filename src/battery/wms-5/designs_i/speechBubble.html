Instructions

Examinee places more designs in the grid than required for the item
A Rule Violation warning sign will appear. If this happens, say, Remember, do not put more than 6 designs in the grid. 

Examineeâ€‹ asks how many designs to place in the grid for an item
You may remind him or her. Do not remind the examinee unless he or she specifically asks.

Examinee places fewer designs in the grid than required for the item
This is not considered a Rule Violation. Do not assist the examinee by reminding him or her of the need to place more designs in the grid. You may remind the examinee only if he or she specifically asks.

Examinee places designs in the perspective of the examiner
Say, Place the designs in the exact way you saw them on the screen, not from my direction.

Examinee does not respond
Encourage him or her to guess. If the examinee does not understand the instructions, say, Choose six designs from down here (point to responses) and move them up here (point to the grid) look like the one you just saw.

Examinee asks for help (other than how many designs to place)
Say, I want to see how well you can do it yourself.

Examinee does not respond or continues to hesitate after a prompt
Say, Let’s go on. Then proceed to the next subtest or item.

Examinee requests repetition or does not appear to understand
Questions or instructions may be repeated, but do not redisplay the stimuli after the initial presentation.
