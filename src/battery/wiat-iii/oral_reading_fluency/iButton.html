Materials 

Client Device (Flat; Portrait View)

Administration

The examinee should begin by reading the first word of the passage, not the title of the passage. Allow the examinee to continue looking at each passage as the questions are asked and answered.

Start
Grade 1: Passage A
Grade 2: Passage C
Grade 3: Passage E
Grade 4: Passage G
Grade 5: Passage I
Grade 6: Passage K
Grades 7–8: Passage M
Grades 9–12+: Passage O

Reverse
Grades 2-12+: If reading time exceeds the reversal time limit on the first passage of the item set, go back one start point and continue administration. The reverse rule may be applied a maximum of 2 times; do not go back more than 2 start points from the grade-appropriate start point.

Stop
Grade 1: After Passage B
Grade 2: After Passage D
Grade 3: After Passage F
Grade 4: After Passage H
Grade 5: After Passage J
Grade 6: After Passage L
Grades 7–8: After Passage N
Grades 9–12+: After Passage P

Timing

For each passage, start timing when the examinee reads the first word of the passage, and stop timing after the examinee reads the last word.

As the examinee is reading, supply (say aloud) unknown words after 5 seconds.

Recording

Don't Know (DK): Used to record when the examinee provides an answer of "I don't know" as his or her only answer

No Response (NR): Used to record when the examinee does not respond

Prompt (P): Used to record when a subtest-specific prompt is provided

Repetition (R): Used to record when the examinee indicates a need for repetition

Scoring

Addition Errors: 
Draw a caret wherever one or more words or phrases are added. Record the added word(s) above the caret. Score each addition as one error, regardless of the number of words added.

Other Errors:
Supplied Words: Record a G wherever a word must be supplied (given) to the student (i.e., after waiting 5 seconds). Score each supplied word as one error.

Substitutions/Omissions: Draw a line through substituted, mispronounced, and omitted words. Record the substituted word(s) above the line. Score each substituted, mispronounced, or omitted word as one error. Substituting a different verb tense (e.g., is eating for eats) is scored as one error.

Transpositions: Draw an S-curve to indicate transposed words. Score each transposition as one error, regardless of the number of words transposed.

Deviations from print that are not recorded as errors:
Contractions: Forming or separating a correct contraction (e.g., it’s for it is or it is for it’s) is not marked as an error.

Self-Corrections: If an error is corrected, it is not marked as an error.

Repetitions: If a word(s) is repeated, it is not marked as an error; record only those errors that occur during the last repetition.

Skipped Line: If a line is skipped, direct the examinee to the correct line, but do not stop timing.

Comprehension Questions:
The comprehension questions are marked correct or incorrect for qualitative evaluation purposes, but they do not contribute to a subtest score.

Refer to the WIAT-III Examiner's Manual for additional information.
