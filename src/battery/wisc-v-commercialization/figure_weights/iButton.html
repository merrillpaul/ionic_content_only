Client Device (Flat)

Administration

Start
Ages 6–8: Sample Item A, then Item 1
Ages 9–16: Sample Item B, then Item 4
Children suspected of having an intellectual disability or low cognitive ability should start with Sample Item A, then Item 1.

Reverse
If a child aged 9–16 does not obtain a perfect score on either of the first two items given, administer the preceding items in reverse order until the child obtains perfect scores on two consecutive items.

Discontinue
After 3 consecutive scores of 0

Timing

The time limit for Items 1–18 is 20 seconds.
The time limit for Items 19–34 is 30 seconds.

Accurate timing is essential. Begin timing for each item after saying the last word of instruction. If the instructions have been eliminated, begin timing after presenting the item.

Stop timing when the child responds, indicates that he or she does not know the answer, or the time limit expires.

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the child provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only response

No Response (NR): Used to record when the child does not respond

Self-Correction (SC): Used to record when the child self-corrects

Subvocalization (SV): Used to record when the child uses observable subvocalization

Scoring

Score 0 or 1 point.

Refer to the WISC–V Administration and Scoring Manual for additional information.
