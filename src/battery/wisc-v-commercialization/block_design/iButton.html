Client Device (Flat) 
Block Design Blocks

Administration

Start
Ages 6–7: Item 1
Ages 8–16: Item 3
Children suspected of having an intellectual disability or low cognitive ability should start with Item 1.

Reverse
If a child aged 8–16 does not obtain a perfect score on either of the first two items given, administer the preceding items in reverse order until the child obtains perfect scores on two consecutive items.

Discontinue
After 2 consecutive scores of 0

Timing

Accurate timing is essential, particularly for items awarded bonus points. Begin timing for each item after saying the last word of instruction.

Stop timing when the child completes the item, the time limit expires, or it is clear from the child’s words or gestures that he or she has finished.

Recording

Number of correctly placed blocks (# Correct Blocks): Used to record the number of correctly placed blocks in the child’s final design

Dimension Error: Used to record when the child’s design violates the standard 2x2 or 3x3 block configuration at any time during construction for Items 2–12

Specify the degree of rotation for each item.

Scoring

Items 1–3: Score 0, 1, or 2 points.
Items 4–9: Score 0 or 4 points.
Items 10–13: Score 0, 4, 5, 6, or 7 points

Refer to the WISC–V Administration and Scoring Manual for additional information.
