Child's stylus is unresponsive
Say, Don't let your hand touch the screen.

Child can't maintain grip on the stylus
Demonstrate an appropriate grip with your stylus and say, Hold it like it's a pen.

Child uses too little or too much pressure when drawing
Say, Try to draw a little (softer, harder).

Child uses their finger instead of the stylus
Say, Use the pen, not your finger.

Child omits item or works items in reverse order
Say, Do them in order. Point to the first omitted item on child's screen and say, Do this one next.

Child asks what to do if they make a mistake
Say, That’s OK. Just keep working as fast as you can. Do not stop timing to provide this prompt.

Child begins working on the demonstration items
Say, I will show you how to do them first.

On sample items, child touches arrow before corrective feedback
Say, Let’s try those again.

Child touches arrow before all items on page are finished
Say, Don't skip any. Point to first omitted item on child's screen and say, Do this one next.

Child does not touch arrow to advance the screen
Say, When you finish a page, touch the arrow and keep working as fast as you can.

Child stops working before time expires
Say, Keep working as fast as you can until you see the check mark.
