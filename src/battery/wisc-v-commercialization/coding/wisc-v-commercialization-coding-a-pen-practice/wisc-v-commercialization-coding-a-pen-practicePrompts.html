Hold up the Examinee Stylus and say, See this pen? You can use it to draw on your screen (draw a few straight and curvy lines in the corner of the Pen Practice box on the child’s screen). The pen is the only thing that should touch the screen. If your hand touches the screen (relax wrist to show secondary hand contact), the pen may stop working.

Hand the child the Examinee Stylus and say, Now you try. Use the pen to draw whatever you want in the box, but make sure the pen is the only thing that touches the screen. Allow the child to practice using the stylus, correcting inappropriate use immediately as follows:

  • If the child’s stylus becomes unresponsive due to secondary hand contact, say, Don’t let your hand touch the screen.

  • If the child has difficulty maintaining an appropriate grip on the stylus, hold up the Examiner Stylus using an appropriate writing grip and say, Hold it like it’s a pen.

  • If the child applies too little or too much pressure to the stylus when drawing, say, Try to draw a little (softer, harder).

If additional practice is necessary, touch the Redo Practice button to clear the drawing area.

When the child has demonstrated adequate stylus use, say, Now you know how to use the pen.

If it is clear that the child will be unable to demonstrate adequate stylus use to complete the subtest, touch the subtest discontinue hand button at the top of the examiner’s screen to discontinue the subtest.
