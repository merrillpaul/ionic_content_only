Instructions
PICTURE ITEMS (1–4)
Provide these queries as often as necessary, but do not query responses that are clearly incorrect:
Marginal responses (e.g., “trash can” to the bucket): Say, Yes, but what else is it called?
 
​​Generalized responses (e.g., “fruit” to the pear): Say, Yes, but what kind of [insert child’s response] is it?
 
Functional descriptions (e.g., “holds water” to the bucket): Say, Yes, but what is it called?
 
Gestures (e.g., pretending to eat in response to the pear): Say, Yes, but what is it called?
VERBAL ITEMS (5–29)
Child asks you to repeat an item
Each item may be repeated as often as necessary, but do not alter the wording in any way.
Child’s response indicates he or she misheard a word
Say, Listen carefully. Repeat the item, emphasizing the misheard word.
Child’s response is unclear, vague, or followed by a Q in the sample responses
Say, What do you mean? or Tell me more about it (or some other neutral inquiry).
Child does not provide a verbal response
Say, Tell me in words what (a, an) [insert stimulus word] (is, means).
