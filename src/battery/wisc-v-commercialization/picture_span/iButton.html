Materials

Client Device (Flat)

Administration

Start
Ages 6–16: Sample Items B & C, then Item 4
Children suspected of having an intellectual disability or low cognitive ability should start with Sample Item A, then Item 1.

Reverse
If a child aged 6–16 does not obtain a perfect score on either of the first two items given, administer the preceding items in reverse order until the child obtains perfect scores on two consecutive items.

Discontinue
After 3 consecutive scores of 0

Timing

Sample Item A–Item 3: Expose the stimulus for 3 seconds.
Sample Item B–Item 26: Expose the stimulus for 5 seconds.

Accurate timing is essential. Timing begins automatically after presenting the stimulus and giving the last word of instruction. Immediately following exposure, provide the indicated instructions.

Recording

Don’t Know (DK): Used to record when the child provides an answer of “I don’t know” as his or her only response

No Response (NR): Used to record when the child does not respond

Self-Correction (SC): Used to record when the child self-corrects

Subvocalization (SV): Used to record when the child uses observable subvocalization

Requested Repetition (RR): Used to record when the child asks for an item to be repeated

Scoring

Items 1–3: Score 0 or 1 point.
Items 4–26: Score 0, 1, or 2 points.

Refer to the WISC–V Administration and Scoring Manual for additional information.
